Core Lesson: Building Positive First Impressions (25 Minutes)

First impressions set the tone. A warm welcome makes customers feel valued. Key tips:

  • Smile genuinely – it's contagious!
  • Greet within 30 seconds: "Hi! Welcome to Canvas Cafe. How many in your party?"
  • Body language: Open posture, eye contact.
  • Personalize: "Great to see you again!" for regulars.
  • Handle waits: "It'll be about 10 minutes – can I get you a menu?"

From expert insights: First impressions influence customer return. Be professional, knowledgeable, and respectful. Read more: Article on First Impressions

Expanded: Employees represent the company. Phone greetings need quick responses; face-to-face require good appearance and tone. Train to be credible and positive.

Extended Learning: More on Impressions (15 Minutes)

Dive deeper: Negative impressions are hard to fix. Focus on manners and problem-solving to retain customers.

Guide students through a group discussion on real-life examples of good and bad first impressions, encouraging sharing of personal stories from restaurants. For extended learning, assign pairs to research and present one tip on improving body language. Take notes on key tips and reflect on how you can apply them in daily interactions, participating actively in discussions to build confidence.

Activity 1: Greeting Role-Play (10 Minutes)

Pair up, act scenarios: happy/grumpy customers. Vote on best greeter.

Prepare scenario cards (e.g., "Customer is in a hurry" or "Family with kids"). Time each role-play to 2 minutes, then debrief as a group on what worked well, providing feedback on body language and tone. Practice varying your tone and expressions, switching roles to experience both sides, noting one thing learned from your partner's performance.

Activity 2: Impression Brainstorm (10 Minutes)

Group brainstorm: List ways to make great first impressions, share examples.

Divide class into small groups (3-4 students). Give 5 minutes for brainstorming, then have each group present top 3 ideas, using a whiteboard to compile all suggestions. Think creatively – include non-verbal cues, being prepared to explain why your idea works.

Module 2: Communication Skills - Talking the Talk (80 Minutes)

Engaging Start: Communication Skit Challenge (10 Minutes)

In small groups, create and perform a 1-minute skit showing a restaurant communication mix-up (e.g., wrong order due to mishearing). Discuss how clear communication could fix it.

Divide students into groups of 3-4, provide simple props (e.g., paper menus). Allow 5 minutes to prepare skits, then 3 minutes for performances, followed by a 2-minute class discussion on lessons learned. Actively participate in your group’s skit creation, ensuring your role highlights a communication error, and reflect on how clarity could improve the outcome.

Core Lesson: Effective Communication (25 Minutes)

Communication prevents errors. Key tips:

  • Listen: Nod, repeat – "Burger no onions, right?"
  • Open questions: "What brings you in?"
  • Positive: "Absolutely!"
  • Tone: Friendly.
  • Specials: "Grilled salmon with veggies."

Watch: Simon Sinek on Great Customer Service

Expanded: Show empathy, try solutions even if not possible. Care about customer goals.

Extended Learning: Advanced Tips (15 Minutes)

More: Distinguish politeness from genuine care. Demonstrate effort in interactions.

Use the video as a springboard for role-playing empathy, having students practice rephrasing negative responses positively, monitoring and providing examples from the expanded content. Watch for non-verbal cues in the video, jotting down phrases you can use in real scenarios to show genuine care.

Activity 1: Whisper Chain (10 Minutes)

Whisper orders, see changes, discuss fixes.

Form a circle with the class, starting with a complex order (e.g., "Vegan burger with extra pickles, no bun, side salad"). After the chain, compare original vs. final, discussing how repeating back prevents errors. Listen carefully and whisper clearly, thinking about how this relates to real restaurant noise.

Activity 2: Communication Role-Play (10 Minutes)

Role-play order taking, focus on listening.

Pairs: One customer with special requests, one server. Use timers for 3-minute scenes, with group feedback on listening techniques used. Use repeat-back and open questions, reflecting on what made communication smooth or tricky.

Core Lesson: Building Effective Teams (25 Minutes)

Teamwork is essential for efficient restaurant operations. Key tips:

  • Hire wisely: Look for team players with positive attitudes.
  • Establish effective communication: Use daily huddles and clear channels.
  • Clarify roles: Ensure everyone knows their responsibilities to avoid overlap.
  • Outline expectations: Set standards for performance and behavior.
  • Encourage collaboration: Foster a supportive environment where help is offered.
  • Provide training: Cross-train staff for flexibility.
  • Recognize efforts: Acknowledge good work to boost morale.
  • Resolve conflicts: Address issues promptly and fairly.

Read more: 8 Tips for Improving Teamwork in the Restaurant Workplace

Expanded: Effective teamwork reduces errors, improves service speed, and enhances customer satisfaction. Focus on building trust and mutual respect among team members.

Extended Learning: Efficiency and Collaboration Strategies (15 Minutes)

Dive deeper: Implement systems for task delegation, use technology for scheduling, and promote a culture of continuous improvement. Discuss real-world examples of successful restaurant teams.

Lead a mind-mapping session on teamwork systems, sharing industry examples from the article, encouraging debate on technology's role. Research one successful restaurant team online briefly and share insights.
